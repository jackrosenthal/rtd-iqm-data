B. Authorization to approve Agreement for the Design, Construction and Special Needs
Maintenance of Southeast Corridor Platform Canopy Enclosures
It is recommended by the Planning and Development Committee that the RTD Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute all documents
necessary to enter into an agreement for design, construction and special needs (nonroutine) maintenance of the Southeast Corridor Platform Canopy Enclosures at Orchard,
Arapahoe, Dry Creek, County Line and Lincoln LRT Stations with the South I-25
Transportation Management Association (“S. I-25 TMA”). FASTER grant funds will be
used for the construction portion of the project. Construction and special needs
maintenance will also be funded by locally-matched S. I-25 TMA funds. RTD will not
contribute any funds toward the construction or special needs maintenance of the Project.
C. Authorization to Enter Into an IGA with CDOT to Accept FASTER Grant Funds for
Central Corridor Light Rail Improvements
It is recommended by the Financial Administration and Audit Committee that the RTD
Board of Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute all
documents necessary to receive FASTER grant funding in the amount of $692,000 from
the Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT), and pay the required local match in
an amount of $173,000. Funding for the four projects is included in the 2012 Proposed
Amended Budget.
D. Authorization to Enter Into an IGA with CDOT to Accept FASTER Grant Funds for
Ticket Vending Machine Upgrades and Replacements
It is recommended by the Financial Administration and Audit Committee that the RTD
Board of Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute all
documents necessary to receive FASTER grant funding in the amount of $440,000 from
the Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT) for ticket vending machine (TVM)
upgrades for the Southeast Corridor and six replacement TVMs for the Southwest
Corridor, and pay the required local match in an amount of $118,041. Funding for the
project is included in the 2012 Adopted Budget.
E. Contract Amendment with Scheidt & Bachmann for Southeast Corridor Ticket Vending
Machine Upgrade and Southwest and Central Corridor Ticket Vending Machine
Replacement
It is recommended by the Financial Administration and Audit Committee that the RTD
Board of Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to increase the existing
contract with Scheidt & Bachmann in the amount of $1,250,703 to upgrade the ticket
vending machines (TVM) on the Southeast Corridor, and to replace the existing TVMs on
the Southwest and Central Corridors. Funding for these projects is included in the 2012
Adopted Budget.
F. Paratransit Service Contract Extensions and Funding
It is recommended by the Operations and Customer Service Committee that the Board
of Directors authorize the General Manager, or his designee, to extend the existing
contracts with First Transit for call center services, Global Transportation, and CUSA
for the provision of Access-a-Ride services and with MV Transportation and VIA
Mobility (formerly Special Transit) for the provision of Access-a-Ride and Call-n-Ride
services for a period of no more than twelve (12) months, at a not-to-exceed cost of
$31,719,781.00 for Access-s-Ride service and $5,437,500.00 for fuel, for a total
not-to-exceed amount of $37,157,281.00 for Access-a-Ride and a not-to-exceed cost

of $3,792,932.00 for Call-n-Ride service and $710,659.00 for fuel, for a total not-toexceed amount of $4,503,591.00 for Call-n-Ride.
G. Recommended Smart Media Technology Fare Enforcement Policy
It is recommended by the Operations and Customer Services Committee that the Board of
Directors approve the recommended smart media technology fare enforcement policy, as
outlined in Attachment A, to take effect upon implementation of Smart Media Technology
fare products beginning January 1, 2013.

H. August 2012 Service Changes
It is recommended by the Operations and Customer Services Committee that the Board of
Directors approve the August 2012 Service Change package as revised and summarized
in Attachment A resulting in an annualized cost increase of approximately $517,000 and,
further, to authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute contract
amendments as necessary for private operators whose service is impacted by these
changes.
XII.

RECOMMENDED ACTION
I. Recommended Smart Media Related Fare Policy Adjustments
It is recommended by the Operations and Customer Services Committee that the Board of
Directors approve the recommended fare policy changes, as outlined in Attachment A, to
take effect upon implementation of Smart Media Technology fare products beginning January
1, 2013.
J. Authorization to Award the Construction Contract for the US 36 Queue Jumps Project
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee that the RTD Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute all documents
necessary to enter into a construction contract with Concrete Express Inc. in the amount
of $2,529,000.00 for the construction of the US 36 Queue Jumps. Funding for this
project is included in the 2012 Adopted Budget for the FasTracks program through a
federal American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) grant.
K. Authorization to Enter Into Agreement with 36 Commuting Solutions and to Amend
Contract with Scheidt & Bachmann for US 36 Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) Ticket Vending
Machines
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee that the RTD Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to execute all documents
necessary to enter into an agreement with 36 Commuting Solutions to receive funds in
the amount of $781,000 to provide ticket vending machines (TVMs) for the US 36 Bus
Rapid Transit (BRT) Project.
It is further recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee that the RTD Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee to amend the existing contract
with Scheidt & Bachmann in the amount of $740,000 to purchase and install the TVMs.
The funds to be received from 36 Commuting Solutions are included in the 2012
Proposed Amended Budget as a third party capital revenue source. Funding to acquire
the TVMs is included in the 2012 Proposed Amended Budget as a separate capital
project.
