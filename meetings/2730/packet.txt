2017 Annual Report Overview
The purpose of the annual report is to capture the initiatives and activities of
the RTD Civil Rights Division.
2017 Highlights of the Year:
• The Civil Rights Division welcomed two new members to the team:
• EEO Manager, Kathryne Grove
• ADA Investigator, Anne Hillyer

• During the 2017 FTA Triennial Review of RTD, the EEO Program and the DBE Program were recognized as model
programs and acknowledged for their utilization of best practices in the transportation industry.
• RTD partnered with the USDOT to host Career Days Discovery on July 17, 2017. This initiative’s intent was to
broaden the horizons of young women to explore careers in the transportation industry.
• Six young girls ages 13-19 had an opportunity to spend a day at RTD learning about careers in transportation and construction
related fields

• Civil Right’s Small Business Office coordinated and hosted the Small Business Breakfast of Champions on
November 9, 2017 to celebrate and recognize the prime contractors, and small, minority, disadvantaged and womenowned businesses that participated on the FasTracks projects throughout the region.

2017 Annual Report Overview
2017 Highlights of the Year continued:
• Civil Right’s Small Business Office, in partnership with the City and County of Denver, CDOT and DEN launched its first
Colorado Small Business Collaboration Conference on September 21, 2017 which reached over 300 attendees
provided workshops and education for small, disadvantaged, minority and women owned businesses.
• VIP presenters presented their perspectives on the future of transportation and opportunities for small, minority, women and
disadvantaged businesses
• VIP Presenters included: Mayor Michael B. Hancock (City & County of Denver), David A. Genova (RTD General Manager and CEO),
Shailen Bhatt (CDOT Executive Director), Eric Hiraga (Executive Director of the Denver Office of Economic Development)

• Civil Right’s ADA Office propelled a community partnership and initiative to promote an inclusive and responsive
environment through the establishment of the Advisory Committee for People with Disabilities (ACPD).
• ACPD shall serve in an advisory capacity and be assisted by a designated staff liaison to the ADA Steering Committee
• ACPD is comprised of 13 members who meet on a quarterly basis
• RTD held an orientation for ACPD members on August 31, 2017

2018 Goals for the Division
Civil Rights Division:
• Continue working on the development of a Diversity class at RTD which will be required for all RTD employees.
• Increase public outreach, initiate a plan to reach out to Denver-area high schools to talk to students about careers
in the transportation industry.
• Coordinate and facilitate Civil Rights Division Overview & Communication Plan meetings to reengage with the
managers of each department in RTD to discuss responsibilities, effective communication, collaborating on
compliance efforts and how we can best support our internal teams.

2018 Goals for the Division continued
Americans with Disabilities Act Office:
• Identify areas/divisions which lack basic understanding of the ADA and provide trainings.
• Continue to review and work with designated divisions to improve RTD's ADA complaint process and
procedures.
• Ensure all ADA complaint policies, processes/procedures are reformatted to comply with RTD's Information
Governance and Management Division new policies.
• Identify trends on ADA complaints received at RTD and work with divisions to come up with ways, means and
solutions to improve and essentially decrease ADA complaints.
• Continue to communicate, provide support and guidance to the various RTD Departments whenever necessary,
in an effort to ensure compliance with ADA requirements.
• Manage and work with the ADA committees at RTD to ensure community involvement in an effort to improve
ADA compliance in all aspects of RTD services, projects and communication.

2018 Goals for the Division continued
Equal Employment Opportunity Office:
• Roll out comprehensive EEO training plan and conduct EEO training throughout the district.
• Partner with Recruiting and Human Resources to assess hiring and promotional practices to ensure compliance
with FTA guidelines.
• Greater outreach throughout the District (i.e. on site office hours, open houses, participating in benefits fair).
• Manage RTD transit related contractors ' EEO compliance requirements including, but not limited to collecting,
reviewing and approving EEO Program Plans, collecting and reviewing workforce analysis, conducting random
site visits, etc.
• Standardize and implement EEO investigation s standard procedures.
• Ensure all EEO complaints are investigated promptly and thoroughly and maintain confidentiality throughout the
investigation to the extent practical and consistent with RTD's obligation.

2018 Goals for the Division continued
Small Business Office:
• Produce an External Annual Report Publication for our community resource partners, associations, and small
businesses. This publication will highlight the previous year's accomplishments, events and upcoming initiatives.
• Continuous improvement in SBE/DBE compliance monitoring and reporting.
• Ensure all contracts monitored by the SBO have complete compliance files with all required pertinent documents
and updated reports and ensure they are in an accessible location and ready for audits at any given time.
• Review and revise the SBE certification procedures by the end of 1st quarter in 2018.
• Conduct timely review and assessment of SBE certification applications.
• Complete and submit all DBE reports to FTA on-time.
• Meet DBE/SBE goal for projects expected to close in 2017.
• Conduct a preliminary assessment of federally funded contracts awarded and projected contracts to be awarded
to ensure we are on track to meeting RTD's Overall DBE Goal of 24%.
