UNANIMOUS CONSENT
B.

Fare Adjustments for Special Services
It is recommended by the Operations/Customer Services Committee that RTD’s fare
structure for Special Services (RockiesRide, BroncosRIde, BuffRide, CU/CSU Football
games at Invesco Field and Race for the Cure) and for all seniorRide services be
adjusted as indicated in Attachment A in order to be consistent with the January 2009
fare structure and to remain in compliance with the Federal Transit Administration
regulations regarding the provision of charter services. It is also recommended that all
future Special Services fares be linked to the RTD fare structure so that in all future
fare changes, all Special Services fares will be changed to reflect the comparable fixed
route fares for similar services.

C.

Approval to exercise three-year option on Contract Award to Strategic Community
Partners for the FasTracks Public Information/Involvement Consultant Contract
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee and the Planning and
Development Committee that the RTD Board of Directors authorize the General
Manager to exercise a three-year option, that will be renegotiated annually, on
the contract award to Strategic Community Partners, the current FasTracks
Public Information/Involvement Consultant, in the amount of $1,098,980 for
Public Information/Involvement, Communications and Public Outreach consulting
work for FasTracks for the year 2009. Funds for the award are contained in the
2009 FasTracks Budget.

G.

Authorization to Accept SB1 Funding
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee and the Planning and
Development Committee that the RTD Board of Directors authorize the General
Manager or his designee to execute all documents necessary to receive Senate Bill 1

demonstration purposes on RTD’s 16th Street Mall Shuttle services.

H.

District-wide Enclaves Annexations and Revised RTD Boundary Map and Description
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee and the Planning and
Development Committee that the Board of Directors authorize publication of a proposed
resolution of annexation and schedule a public hearing and consideration of the
resolution at the Board meeting of December 16, 2008. Publication of the proposed
resolution will initiate the process to annex several parcels of unincorporated land
surrounded by the District in accordance with the provisions of CRS 32-9-106.8.

I.

Proposed Modification to RTD Investment Policy
It is recommended by the Financial/Administration Committee that the RTD Investment
Policy be modified to include the following language in Section 7 (Eligible Investments):
Should an owned security fall below minimum credit ratings required at time of
purchase, the Manager of Debt and Investments shall, as a prudent
professional, disclose and manage the risks associated with the security.

J.

Recommendation for Union Pacific Railroad Property Acquisition and Relocation
Agreement
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee that the Board of Directors
authorize the General Manger and his designees to negotiate one or more agreements
with the Union Pacific Railroad (UPRR) for the acquisition of property and relocation of
certain railroad improvements for the East, Gold, North Metro and West Corridor
(Burnham Lead) lines according to the general term and price parameters set forth in
this action. It is further recommended that the General Manager be authorized to spend
up to $185 million for the property and relocation projects specified below, distributed
as shown. Any redistribution of funding among the acquisitions and projects by more
than 5% for any item will require further Board action, provided such redistribution may
not increase the total cost.

K.

Proposed Modification to RTD Interest Rate Swap Policy, Annual Report to the Board –
Interest Rate Swaps
It is recommended by the Financial/Administration Committee that the RTD Interest
Rate Swap Policy be modified to include the following Section IX:
IX.

Ethics and Conflicts of Interest:
Activities related to the procurement and management of interest rate exchange
agreements shall be performed in full accordance with the District’s Code of
Ethics and other policies of the District. Officers and employees of the District
involved in the process of procuring and managing interest rate swaps shall
refrain from personal business activity that could conflict with the proper
execution of these financial products, or which could impair their ability to make
impartial decisions related thereto. Employees and District officials shall
disclose to the General Manager any material financial interests in financial
institutions that conduct business with the District, and they shall further
disclose any large personal/financial/investment positions that could be related
to the performance of the District’s swap portfolio.
