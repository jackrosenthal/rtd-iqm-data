Denver Transit Construction Group Early Procurement/Early Construction Change
Order
It is recommended by the Planning and Development Committee that the RTD Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager to amend contract number 16-FF-W01 for the
Denver Transit Construction Group, the West Corridor Construction Manager/General
Contractor (CMGC) to allow for:
1. Procurement and fabrication of bridge steel girders for bridges at Indiana,
Wadsworth and Consolidated Mainline.
2. Procurement and fabrication of steel for bridge trusses at 6th Avenue (US6)
and S. Platte River.
3. Procurement and fabrication of raw copper for signal cables, switch cable,
substation positive and negative return feeder cables, impedance bond cable
and cross bonding cable.
4. Early construction bid and construction of bridges for light rail fly-overs at
Kipling Street and the east and west Dry Gulch bridges.
The cost of this change order is not to exceed $29,000,000.00 including contingency.
Funding for this work is available in the West Corridor budget and has been approved
through a Letter of No Prejudice (LONP) request under Federal Transit Administration
(FTA) guidelines. The LONP was received from FTA on September 25, 2007.

N.

Award of access-a-Ride Paratransit Services-revised
It is recommended by the Contracted Operational Services Committee that the Board of
Directors authorize the General Manager or his designee, to enter into contracts with
four (4) service providers, MV Transportation, Global Transportation, CUSA, and
Special Transit, for the provision of access-a-Ride Paratransit Services. These contracts
are for three years with two (2) one year options. The combined amounts for the initial
three (3) year contract terms shall not exceed $79,516,724. The combined amounts
for the two (2) one-year options shall not exceed $32,298,512 total in option year 1
and $35,816,824 total in option year 2. The combined total amounts for these
contracts shall not exceed $147,632,060 for the five year period. In addition, RTD will
reimburse the contractors’ direct fuel costs in amounts estimated to total approximately
$14,745,200 for the initial three year contracts and $26,670,700 for the five year
period. This results in combined contract totals not to exceed $94,261,924 in total for
the initial three year contracts and $174,302,760 for the five year period. These
proposals represent the most responsive and responsible proposals received in response
to Request for Proposals (RFP) 17-DH-007, to provide Paratransit Services. The
contracts to provide this service are scheduled to be effective January 1, 2008.

U.

Purchase of Fifty-Five (55) Light Rail Vehicles
It is recommended by the FasTracks Monitoring Committee that the Board of Directors
authorize the General Manager or his designee to negotiate a sole source contract with
Siemens Transportation Group, Inc. (Siemens) for the procurement of fifty-five (55)
light rail vehicles to meet future transportation needs of the West Corridor, the
extensions planned in FasTracks for the Southwest, the Southeast, the I-225 and the
Central Corridors, and the enhancements to the Southwest and Southeast Corridors
planned in FasTracks. In addition, RTD will continue to find ways to better
accommodate bike, strollers, wheelchairs, etc. on existing high floor equipment,
carefully monitor FTA changes in vehicle technology as it relates to low floor vehicles
and ensure full compliance with those requirements. RTD will also continue to work
with Operators and Passengers to address accessibility issues with current
configuration, continue to explore options that may offer low floor access for at least
one exit/entrance to each train, and to come to a better understanding of additional
costs incurred presently by using high floor vehicles in terms of costs of ramp
construction and additional dwell time that occurs at each station.
