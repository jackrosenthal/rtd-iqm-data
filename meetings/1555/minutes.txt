GFOA Recognition
At the beginning of the meetings, Committee Chair James read the Certificate of
Achievement for Excellence in Financial reporting awarded to Regional Transportation
District by the Government finance Officers Association of the United States and Canada
(GFOA) for its comprehensive annual financial report (CAFR). The Certificate of
Achievement is the highest form of recognition in the area of governmental accounting and
financial reporting, and its attainment represents a significant accomplishment by a
government and its management.
Terry Howerter, CFO, accepted the award on behalf of his team and introduced Sue

V.

December Financial Status report
Janette Scarpino introduced this item. She made references to the dashboard on page 44
that shows monthly and year-to-date comparisons on ridership, fare revenue and sales
and use tax. The year to date shows ridership down by .9% and fare revenue and sales
and use tax up by 1.0% and 7.1% respectively.
She also referenced the cost recovery ratios on page 49. Director O’Boyle asked for an
explanation of the cost recovery ratios. Jannette provided the formula used but stated
She will get him additional information on the ratio itself per C.R.S.A. § 32-9-119.7
Janette walked through page 50 pointing out variances in different areas of interest (i.e.
outside services, miscellaneous expenses, Federal grant revenue, miscellaneous income).
Director Brohl inquired about the variance on outside services and questioned why it could
not be accrued or moved to the 2011 budget. Terry Howerter indicated he planned to do
so.
Chair Kemp asked for an update on diesel fuel and when will we start to look at the lock
for 2012. General Manager Washington responded in March or April but he has a man
who watches the markets continuously.
