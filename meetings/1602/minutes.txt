2.

January Financial Status Report
Robert Merriman, Acting Manager of Budget and Financial Analysis reported on the
January Financial Status Report.
 Total monthly boardings were 5.3% higher in January 2007 than in January 2006.
 Combined Base and FasTracks sales tax receipts were 2.5% higher than January
2006 actual receipts and -1.4% lower than the 2007 Adopted Budget forecast for
the month.
 Combined Base and FasTracks use tax receipts were 12.6% higher than January
2006 actual receipts and 0.2% higher than the 2007 Adopted Budget forecast for
the month.
 Total Farebox Revenues collected were 9.7% higher in January 2007 than they
were in January 2006 which is primarily due to the increase in Ridership.

3.

Refunding Results – 2007A Sales Tax Revenue Refunding Bonds
Teresa Sedmak, Manager of Debt and Investments, discussed this item.
On March 6, 2007, 2007A Sales Tax Revenue Refunding Bonds were priced by Goldman
Sachs. As a result of favorable market conditions, the District was able to refund
approximately $75 million in outstanding bonds. The cash savings to the District as a
result of this transaction total approximately $6.08 million over the life of the transaction
(through November 1, 2024) or $3.6 million on a present value basis. This savings
represent 4.83% of the refunded bonds.

4.

ERP Update
Pat Seifert, Project Manager of ERP introduced the RTD Staff and Team Ciber to the
Financial/Administrative Committee. Pat gave on overview of the ERP Four-Phase
Implementation, which consisted of:
Phase 1 – Went live January 2007. Includes Financials, budget, project/grants.
Phase 1.5 – Will go live September 2007. Includes: Maintenance management and
purchasing inventory.
Phase 2 –Will go live January 2008. Includes: Payroll, Human Resources, and Benefits.
Phase 3 – Will go live July 2008. Includes: Training Management, business intelligence.
For Phase 1 – all project goals were met.
The next scheduled update to the Financial/Administration Committee will be done in June
of 2007.
